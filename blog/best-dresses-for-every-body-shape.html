Best Dresses for Every Body Shape

Every woman deserves to feel confident in what she wears. The key is to choose outfits that highlight your body’s natural proportions. Here’s how to pick dresses for each shape:

Hourglass

Opt for wrap dresses or belted outfits that highlight your defined waistline.

Pear

A-line skirts and off-shoulder tops balance your lower and upper halves beautifully.

Apple

Empire waist dresses and V-necks create a long, balanced silhouette.

Rectangle

Ruffled and peplum dresses add curves to your shape, giving the illusion of a waist.

Banana

For a banana (or rectangle) body shape, the goal is to create the illusion of curves and define the waist. Look for dresses with ruching around the waist, belted or banded designs, and tie-waist pieces to cinch in the midsection. A-line dresses, empire-style cuts, and fit-and-flare dresses also help create a more feminine silhouette. Dresses with ruffles, bows, or embellishments can add volume, particularly to the bust or hip areas. Halter necklines, one-shoulder, and off-shoulder styles can create the illusion of wider shoulders.

Bell

For a bell body shape (also known as pear or triangle), the goal is to create balance by emphasizing the upper body and choosing styles that flatter the lower half without adding bulk. A-line dresses, fit-and-flare dresses, and wrap dresses are highly recommended. Strapless dresses, dresses with embellished or voluminous sleeves (like cap sleeves, puff sleeves), and cowl necklines help to broaden the shoulders and draw attention upwards. Dresses with deep waistbands or those paired with wide belts can help define the waist.

Inverted Triangle

For an inverted triangle body shape, the most flattering dresses are those that aim to balance broader shoulders by adding volume and drawing attention to the lower body. A-line dresses, fit-and-flare dresses, shift dresses, and empire waist dresses are excellent choices. V-necks, scoop necks, and U-necks gracefully streamline the upper body. Look for dresses with flared skirts, pleats, ruffles, or other details at the hips. Flowy fabrics like chiffon, silk, or rayon for the skirt drape beautifully and add movement and volume to the hips.

Triangle

For a triangle body shape (also known as pear shape), the goal is to balance the wider hips and thighs with the narrower shoulders. A-line and fit-and-flare dresses are highly recommended as they cinch at the waist and gently flare out over the hips. Wrap dresses are excellent for accentuating the waist and creating a flattering V-neckline. Belted styles help to highlight the narrowest part of the torso. Off-the-shoulder, boat neck, sweetheart, and square necklines help to broaden the appearance of the shoulders and draw attention upwards.

Spoon

For a spoon body shape, the key is to balance proportions by emphasizing the upper body and highlighting the waist. A-line dresses, empire waist dresses, fit-and-flare dresses, and baby doll dresses are highly recommended. Open necklines like V-neck, scoop neck, and square neck tops are excellent choices. Off-the-shoulder, strapless, boat-neck, and cowl-neck styles draw attention to your shoulders, collarbone, and neckline. Bell sleeves can also enhance the bustline.

X-Shape

For an X-shape body type (often referred to as an hourglass figure), the key is to highlight the natural waistline and maintain the balanced proportions of the bust and hips. Fitted dresses, wrap dresses, fit-and-flare dresses, belted dresses, bodycon dresses, and sheath dresses are excellent choices. V-neck and scoop neck styles work well as they draw attention towards the collarbones. Fitted sleeves, set-in sleeves, cap sleeves, and Bishop sleeves are good choices, as is a classic sleeveless style.
